OVERVIEW

Deck Drills is a mobile app that empowers users to learn new vocabulary in a fun and engaging way. It was developed as part of the CareerFoundry Fundamentals course.

MY ROLE

I was responsible for all stages of the design process, from the initial concept and problem definition, to the implementation of the final user interface.

PROJECT DURATION

2 months

THE PROJECT

Problem Statement

The design​ ​of a​ ​mobile​ ​app​ ​that​ ​empowers​ ​people​ ​to​ ​learn​ ​new​ ​vocabulary.

Challenges

  • People need an intuitive and easy-to-use way to create and customize their own flashcards;
  • ​The​ ​experience​ ​of​ ​memorizing​ ​and​ ​understanding​ ​new concepts,​ ​techniques,​ ​and​ ​terms can feel overwhelming, so people need a way to feel motivated and excited to learn.

Opportunities

  • Develop a learning app that is intuitive and simple to use.
  • Provide instant feedback and progress checks to help users effectively and efficiently learn new concepts and material.
  • A key component of learning is enthusiasm, therefore it is important that the app is fun and enjoyable to keeps users motivated and excited to learn.

THE FIVE W's

WHO?

The age range of the target audience is anyone between 14 - 45 years old looking to learn new vocabulary, either for school, work, self-developmnt of simply for fun.

WHAT?

A mobile application focused on learning new vocabulary.

WHERE?

This application is meant to be used on-the-go for a quick 5–10-minute study sessions. The users could use it on the train, on a lunch break or even before bedtime.

WHEN?

Location and context will ultimately depend on the target users. That could be on a bus or train, waiting between classes, in the cafe, in bed, or on a bus.

WHY?

It can be challenging to keep track of all the jargon when studying a new discipline. Students need a way to methodically categorize, reference, and explore new vocabulary and concepts so they can move forward confidently in their field.

KEY USER RESEARCH FINDINGS

  • It is important for learning apps to incorporate all modes of learning including kinetic, repetition, visual, word association, and experiential learning to suit all types of learners.
  • People are motivated to learn when they can see their progress and, in particular, when they are able to use what they’ve learnt in a real-world setting, e.g. when learning a new language they get excited when they can start conversing with a native speaker.
  • Learning apps offer users the convenience and flexibility to study when they want and in an environment of their choosing.
  • Interviewees mentioned that learning through an app is limited, particularly if it is the only mode used to discover a new field. It is more suited to simply memorizing material, rather than for understanding new concepts due to the lack of interactivity with an instructor or other students.
  • Making learning fun through games and competition can be a successful technique to encourage students to use a leearning app, however it should not be the main focus of the app since it can distract users from the material.
  • Many interviewees highlighted the importance of an immediate feedback loop when it comes to learning a new subject, not simply in terms of informing whether an answer if correct or not, but, more importantly, in providing an explanation for why. Indeed, the lack of instant feedback or inability to ask questions was cited as a major cause for frustration with virtual learning.

USER STORIES

As a busy working professional, I want to be able to learn in a flexible environment so that I can study at my convenience.

As an avid note-taker, I want to be able to digitally upload my notes so that they can be accessible and searchable in an organized fashion.

As a student, I want to be able to learn in a fun and engaging way so that I remain motivated and excited to learn.

As a visual and practical learner, I want to combine audio, visual and written modes to help make learning more permanent as this involves the greatest number of senses during the learning process.
