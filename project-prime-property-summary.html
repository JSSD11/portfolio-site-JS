OVERVIEW

Prime Property is a responsive web app that provides property buyers with with information on properties of interest. The app also connects people with real estate agents to make the process of buying a new home as easy as possible.

MY ROLE

As the UI designer, I was responsible for setting the visual direction for the project and delivered mockups of varied fidelity, along with a final prototype.

PROJECT DURATION

2 months

THE PROJECT

Real estate investment is an increasingly popular way for individuals to achieve financial security. It is an exciting and emotional experience, but often complicated. While there are plenty of blogs and agencies providing information, often, buyers new to the market may struggle to get started without professional guidance and waste time viewing properties out of their range. This web app will provide them with the expertise needed to get started efficiently.

TOOLS

Sketchpad, pen & pencil, Adobe Illustrator, Adobe Photoshop, Sketch, Adobe XD, Adobe After Effects.

The Five W's

Who?

This web app is made primarily for new, small-scale property buyers who are looking to invest for additional income or financial security.

What?

This will be a user-friendly, responsive web app containing a database of available residential properties and land, and comprehensive information on each listing.

When?

Buyers will use this tool when conducting property searches, and making a decision about where to invest.

Where?

Buyers will use this tool at home or on the go. Users can search for properties anywhere, as long as theyâ€™re logged in on a device.

Why?

Unseasoned buyers need access to reliable, uncomplicated information about their potential property investments. Buyers get a feel for a place by viewing comprehensive information about the property and its neighborhood before spending time on-site.

Initial Research

Much of the initial research had already been provided. In order to build upon the initial research information, I conducted a competitive analysis to better understand the current solutions available and assess how successful they were at addressing the problem, and hence spot opportunity gaps in the market.

Following the initial research, I noted a few ideas and elements that stood out to me:

  • The common features of real estate apps include:
    • User profiles: Users should be able to create their profiles in the app so that they can perform different activities which suit their needs.
    • Search filters and categories: To simplify the property searching process, the app should provide advanced search options to users so that they can easily locate properties that suit their needs.
    • Property listing profile: These profiles provide comprehensive information about the property, such as description, photos, videos, price details, value proposition, neighborhood information, agency information etc.
    • Contact agent: Providing users with in-depth property information is just one part - the app should take users to the next stage of sales funnel by providing users with the option to contact the dealer through a call or direct messaging feature.
    • Favourites (Shortlisting): The app should allow users to shortlist and save their favorite properties, as this helps to make their decision-making process much easier.
    • Create and save search profile: Allowing users to create and save search profiles according to specific filter categories greatly simplifies the search process.
    • Create and save search profile: Allowing users to create and save search profiles according to specific filter categories greatly simplifies the search process.
    • Maps: Integrating maps in the app can help provide lots of valuable information to end-users.
    • Notifications: Notifications that provide value to the end-users are vital to retain and stay on top of the customerâ€™s mind. It is helpful to notify users of a price change or when a new property is added to their saved search profiles. Users must opt in to receiving such notifications.
  • Notes on visual design:
    • Most real-estate apps use vibrant colours, with lots of greens and blues.most real-estate apps use vibrant colours, with lots of greens and blues.
    • The pages tend to be dense with information, and often look cluttered.
    • The minimalist design trend of simplicity, clean lines, and white space, which has been popular in architecture and interior since the 2000â€™s, has yet to catch on with real estate brands, which, for the most part, remain stodgy and dated.

Sketches and Low-Fidelity Prototype

Following the research phase, I began brainstorming my own solutions with pen and sketchbook. These sketches allowed me to start working on the basic layout of each screen and visualize the various steps the user would take to achieve the goals laid out in the user flow.

I kept the wireframes to a low level of detail, and simply test the interactions and overall structure of the ideas.

Responsive Design

The project brief called for the design of a responsive web appThe project brief called for the design of a responsive web app. Making the design responsive means that the design should look good on multiple design interfaces. This is achieved by designing for mulitple breakpoints.Making the design responsive means that the design should look good on multiple design interfaces, which is achieved by designing for mulitple breakpoints.

The decison on which breakpoints to design for was made by considering the content, i.e. depending on where the mobile design started to break and look fragmented, incohesive, and inaccessible on larger breakpoints, as well as considering the most popular device sizes used. It was thus decided to have three different design breakpoints - one for mobile at 375px, one for portrait tablet at 768px, and one for large desktop at 1280px.
