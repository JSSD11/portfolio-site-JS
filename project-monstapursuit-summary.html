OVERVIEW

MonstaPursuit is a mobile app that turns your run into a game, where you must find and battle virtual monsters with exercise along your route. "Challenges" can be completed alone or with friends, making the game a competitive experience.

The app provides a new way to bring variety and fun into running so as to keep users motivated to exercise, as well as a social platform to foster local fitness communities.

MY ROLE

All stages of the design process: initial concept; UX design; visual design

PROJECT DURATION

5 months

THE PROJECT

I designed MonstaPursuit as part of CareerFoundry’s UX Design course, where I was tasked to design a scavenger hunt mobile app. I decided to expand the concept of typical scavenger hunt apps, which tend to be geared towards the tourist or team-building markets, and instead focus on fitness and exercise. Hence, the idea behind MonstaPursuit is to turn a one-dimensional, individual workout, such as running,into a multi-dimensional, fun, and social experience.

THE CHALLENGE

  • Make running a fun and social activity.
  • Provide a way to monitor and view progress.
  • Provide a motivation system that encourages people to reach their goals.
  • Motivate people to be develop healthy habits and exercise regularly.

THE SOLUTION

  • A game where players earn points by battling virtual monsters with exercise along their run.
  • Allow players to challenge each other and share their experience.
  • Players can easily view and analyse their stats.
  • Reward players with points that can be exchanged for real rewards.

KEY USER RESEARCH FINDINGS

  • Respondents had mixed impressions on the competitive nature of fitness apps; some are motivated by competing with themselves and friends, while others find it demoralizing.
  • Scavenger hunts were recognized as a great group activity for team-building, shared experience etc. but people rarely partook in them - they were viewed as one-off events.
  • Social features play an important role in motivation (e.g. connecting with like-minded people, peer-recognition, finding inspiration & new ideas), but not all respondents were keen to share their own results.
  • What makes exercise a habit: routine; planning ahead; sense of accomplishment; seeing progress; goal setting; variety to prevent boredom and fitness plateau; the feel-good feeling after exercise.

USER STORIES

As someone new to exercise, I would like a way to encourage myself to get active outdoors, so that I can make lasting changes and improve my overall health.

As a fitness enthusiast, I would like to be able to make my regular run more challenging by working other core muscle groups so that I can get fitter and stronger.

As someone pursuing a health goal, I would like to message friends on similar goals so that we can keep each other accountable and motivate one another.

As a keen competitor, I would like to be able to compare new results with historic results so that I can see progress which keeps me motivated.
